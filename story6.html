Why the Hawk Hunts Chicks

L ong ago, the Hawk and the Hen were best friends who lived together in the same compound. They would share meals, watch each other's children, and help each other with daily tasks. The Hawk was known for her excellent cooking, while the Hen was praised for her skillful farming. One day, the Hawk had to travel to a distant village for her sister's wedding. She asked her friend Hen to look after her three eggs until she returned. "Of course!" said Hen, "Your eggs will be as safe as my own chicks." The Hawk thanked her friend and flew away. That evening, as Hen was preparing dinner, she accidentally knocked over a pot of hot soup. The soup spilled onto the nest where she had placed Hawk's eggs, cooking them instantly. Hen was devastated but too afraid to tell Hawk the truth. When Hawk returned a week later and asked about her eggs, Hen lied, saying they had been stolen by the Monitor Lizard. But as Hawk looked around, she noticed eggshell pieces in Hen's cooking area and realized what had happened. She wasn't angry about the accident, but the lie broke her heart. "From this day forward," declared Hawk, "our friendship is over. And because you couldn't tell me the truth about my children, I will forever hunt yours." She flew away in sorrow. This is why, to this day, when a hawk appears in the sky, mother hens quickly gather their chicks under their wings and remember the cost of a broken trust between friends.

Moral: Honesty, even in difficult situations, is better than a lie that breaks trust forever.
