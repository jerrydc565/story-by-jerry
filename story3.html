The Magic Palm Tree

I n a small village near the Niger River lived a young girl named Adanna who lost both her parents at a very young age. She lived with her stepmother and stepsister, who treated her poorly, making her do all the hard work while they relaxed in the shade. One day, while fetching water from the river, Adanna met an old woman who was struggling to cross. Despite her own fatigue, Adanna helped the woman across and shared her last piece of yam with her. The old woman, who was a spirit in disguise, was touched by Adanna's kindness. "My child," she said, "take this palm kernel and plant it behind your compound. But remember - you must water it with your tears and speak to it with love." Adanna did as she was told. Each morning and evening, while doing her chores, she would cry near the palm kernel and share her sorrows with it. Soon, a beautiful palm tree grew, but this was no ordinary tree. Whenever Adanna sang to it, the tree would drop delicious palm fruits and beautiful gifts - fine clothes, jewelry, and even food. Her stepmother and stepsister, noticing Adanna's sudden good fortune, spied on her and discovered the secret. The stepsister tried to copy Adanna, but when she sang to the tree, instead of gifts, she was showered with dirt and thorns. Angry, the stepmother ordered the tree to be cut down. But that night, the spirit appeared again to Adanna and told her to take a single leaf from the fallen tree. This leaf turned into an even more magnificent palm tree that would only respond to Adanna's voice. From that day forward, Adanna prospered and eventually became a wealthy and respected woman in her village. She never forgot her humble beginnings and always helped others in need.

Moral: Kindness, patience, and a pure heart will always be rewarded, while greed and cruelty bring their own punishment
