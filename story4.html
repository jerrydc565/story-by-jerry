The Dancing Drum

I n a village famous for its music lived a young boy named Olayinka, who was born unable to speak. Though he couldn't talk, Olayinka had a special gift - his hands could create the most beautiful rhythms on any drum he touched. His father, the village's master drummer, had given him a special drum made from an ancient tree. Every evening, Olayinka would sit at the village square, playing his drum. The music was so enchanting that even the birds would stop to listen. But there was one thing peculiar about his drum - whenever danger was approaching the village, the drum would start playing on its own, warning everyone. One year, during the harvest festival, a group of bandits planned to raid the village. They chose this time because they knew everyone would be celebrating and off guard. As night fell and the village slept, Olayinka's drum began to play by itself - a frantic, urgent rhythm that woke the entire village. At first, people were angry at being awakened, but the village chief recognized the warning in the rhythm. He quickly organized the warriors, and they caught the bandits by surprise. The village was saved! But something even more magical happened that night. As Olayinka played a victory song on his drum, his voice suddenly returned! The village medicine man explained that the ancient tree from which the drum was made had been waiting for someone pure of heart to break its spell. From that day forward, Olayinka could both speak and play his drum. But he never forgot the lesson he learned - that there are many ways to communicate, and sometimes the most powerful messages don't need words at all.

Moral: Everyone has a special gift, and what some might see as a limitation could be a blessing in disguise.
