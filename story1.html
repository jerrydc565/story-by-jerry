Why The Tortoise Has a Cracked Shell

L ong ago, in a time when all the animals lived together in harmony, there was a great drought in the land. The animals were hungry, and food was scarce. One day, the birds decided to help by flying up to the sky, where they knew of a grand feast being held by their friends, the sky-dwellers. The clever Tortoise, hearing of this plan, begged the birds to let him join them. "But Tortoise," they said, "you have no wings!" The resourceful Tortoise suggested that each bird give him one feather, and together they could make him a pair of wings. The kind-hearted birds agreed. Before they left, Tortoise suggested that they each take new names for the feast, as was the custom in those days. He chose for himself the name "All of You." When they arrived at the feast, their hosts announced that the food was prepared "for all of you." Tortoise, being clever but greedy, claimed that since his name was "All of You," all the food belonged to him! He ate and ate while the birds watched hungrily. Angry at his trickery, when it was time to return home, the birds took back their feathers. Tortoise, stranded in the sky, begged them to at least tell his wife to put soft things outside his house so he could land safely. The birds, still bitter about his deception, told his wife to put out hard objects instead. When Tortoise fell from the sky, his once-smooth shell crashed against the hard ground, breaking into many pieces. Spider, who lived nearby, helped piece him back together, but could never make the shell smooth again. And that is why, to this day, the tortoise's shell has many crack-like patterns.

Moral: Greed and deception may bring temporary gain, but often lead to lasting consequences.
