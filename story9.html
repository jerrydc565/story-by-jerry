The Talking Yam

I n the days when yams could speak, there lived a poor farmer named Obiora who owned just a small plot of land. Unlike his neighbors who had vast farms, Obiora treated each of his yams with special care, talking to them every day as he worked his field. One morning, while digging up his harvest, he was startled to hear a yam talk back! This yam was larger than the others and had unusual golden-brown skin that seemed to shimmer in the sunlight. "Obiora," the yam spoke, "because you have shown kindness to all your crops, I will grant you a wish. But remember - a greedy wish brings grief, while a wise wish brings joy." Obiora thought carefully. His first instinct was to wish for a bigger farm or more yams. But as he looked at his small but healthy field, he remembered how his grandfather always said, "The sweetest yam is the one you've nurtured yourself." So instead, he wished for the knowledge to help other farmers grow better crops. The magic yam glowed brightly and dissolved into a shower of golden dust that spread across the land. From that day forward, Obiora discovered he could understand the whispers of all plants. He learned when they were thirsty, when they needed more sun, and when they were ready for harvest. He shared this knowledge freely with other farmers, teaching them to listen to their crops and care for them properly. Soon, the entire village's harvest improved. People came from far and wide to learn from Obiora. Though his farm remained small, his wisdom helped feed countless families, and he became known as the happiest farmer in all the land.

Moral: True wealth lies not in how much we own, but in how much we can help others grow.
