The First Rainy Season

O nce, in a time when rain was unknown to Earth, all the water in the world was held by a great king in Heaven. The Earth was dry and dusty, and plants could only grow near rivers and streams. People had to travel long distances just to find water for their basic needs. In the village closest to Heaven lived a brave young woman named Amara, who was known for her beautiful singing voice. Every evening, she would sit outside her hut and sing melodies so beautiful that even the birds would stop to listen. Her songs carried stories of the Earth's thirst and the struggles of her people. One day, her songs reached the ears of the king's daughter in Heaven. Moved by Amara's beautiful voice and the sadness of her songs, the princess would secretly lean over the edge of Heaven to listen. During one such evening, her father's precious water jar slipped from Heaven's edge and shattered upon hitting Earth. The water from the jar spread across the sky, creating clouds for the first time. As the clouds grew heavy, they began to release water in small drops - the first rain Earth had ever seen. The plants drank thirstily, and the people danced in joy. The king of Heaven was initially angry, but when he saw how the rain transformed the Earth - making it green and fertile - he realized that his water was meant to be shared. From that day forward, he ordered the clouds to regularly visit Earth with their precious rain. Some say that even today, when you hear thunder, it's Amara, still singing her songs of gratitude. The rumbling is her voice echoing through the clouds

Moral: The power of music and compassion can break even the strongest barriers, and sometimes accidents can lead to the greatest blessings.
