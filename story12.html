The Tree That Spoke Truth

I n the heart of an ancient forest stood a massive iroko tree unlike any other. Its branches stretched so high they seemed to touch the clouds, and its trunk was wider than ten men standing hand in hand. But what made this tree truly special was its unique power - anyone who told a lie while standing beneath its branches would immediately turn purple until they spoke the truth. The tree became known as the Justice Tree, and the village chief would hold all important meetings beneath its branches. One year, during a terrible drought, food began disappearing from the village storehouse. The chief gathered everyone under the Justice Tree to find the culprit. Among those gathered was Obioma, the chief's favorite advisor, who always wore elaborate robes and claimed to have magic powers. When questioned about the missing food, Obioma confidently declared, "I know nothing about the missing food. In fact, I've been fasting for the past month to bring rain to our land!" Instantly, Obioma turned a deep shade of purple. The crowd gasped as his elaborate robes suddenly seemed less magnificent. Under the tree's power, the truth came tumbling out - he had been stealing food and selling it to a neighboring village. But then something unexpected happened. As Obioma confessed and showed genuine remorse, the tree's leaves began to rustle and drop small golden fruits. These fruits, when planted, grew into crops that could survive the drought. The tree had turned Obioma's dishonesty into a blessing for the entire village. The chief, showing wisdom, declared that Obioma would repay his debt by teaching others how to grow these new crops. From that day forward, people said that the greatest power of the Justice Tree was not in revealing lies, but in turning wrongs into opportunities for redemption.

Moral: Truth may sometimes be difficult to speak, but it carries within it the seeds of healing and growth.
